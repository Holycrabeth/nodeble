Half-Kelly captures ~75% of optimal growth with ~50% less drawdown.\n\nFor an iron condor system with known win rate (say 75% with 50% profit targets) and known reward/risk (say 1:2, collecting $1 credit with $2 max loss), Half-Kelly determines the optimal number of simultaneous positions.\n\nThe hybrid approach from the research combines Kelly with VIX-based scaling: in low VIX, Kelly suggests larger positions (edge is reliable); in high VIX, Kelly suggests smaller positions (variance is higher despite richer premium).\n\n**Impact (High):** Position sizing is the single biggest determinant of long-term returns and drawdown magnitude. Moving from fixed sizing (e.g., always 2 contracts) to Kelly-informed sizing (more contracts when the edge is large and reliable, fewer when uncertain) meaningfully improves the growth/drawdown tradeoff.\n\n**Complexity (Medium):** The formula is simple. The hard part is estimating the inputs accurately: win rate and reward/risk must be estimated from rolling recent history (not all-time, because market regime changes). Update the Kelly estimate monthly using the trailing 6-month performance window.\n\n**Automatable:** Fully. Compute Half-Kelly fraction, convert to contract count, cap at the existing position limits.\n\n**Pitfalls:** Kelly assumes independent bets, but iron condor outcomes are correlated (if SPY crashes, all equity condors lose simultaneously). The fix is to apply Kelly to the portfolio as a whole (total capital allocated to all positions) rather than to each position individually. Also, Kelly with bad input estimates can be worse than fixed sizing -- hence Half-Kelly as a buffer.\n\n**Verdict: High priority. Implement in two steps: (1) Start logging win rate and avg win/loss ratio immediately. (2) After 6 months of data, switch from fixed position count to Half-Kelly-informed sizing with a portfolio-level cap.**\n\n---\n\n### E4. Gamma Exposure Monitoring Near Expiration\n\n**Concept:** Gamma accelerates exponentially inside 14 DTE. A position that was safely 5 deltas from the money can swing to 25+ deltas with a 1% underlying move. The consensus is: close all positions at 21 DTE or earlier if gamma exceeds a threshold (e.g., absolute gamma > 0.05 on any short strike).\n\n**Impact (High):** This is a risk prevention measure, not a profit enhancer. It prevents the rare but devastating scenario where a position enters the \"gamma vortex\" in the final week and a modest move causes max loss.\n\n**Complexity (Low):** Monitor gamma on short strikes daily. This should already exist if the system has a DTE-based close trigger, but adding explicit gamma monitoring adds a second safety net.**\n\n---\n\n## F) Market Microstructure\n\n### F1. Over 100+ trades per year, this compounds to a meaningful P&L difference.\n\n**Complexity (Medium):** The sequential leg execution system already handles individual leg orders. Adding mid-price targeting with incremental walk logic requires order management sophistication.
